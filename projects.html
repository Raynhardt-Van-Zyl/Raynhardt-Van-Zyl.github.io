Robotics

Heterogeneous Computing for Low-Cost Robotic Platforms
Raynhardt van Zyl and Dr Callen Fisher

This study presents a novel approach to the design of cost-effective autonomous rovers using a heterogeneous computing architecture. The research focuses on the development and evaluation of an autonomous rover system, priced under R50 000, that can operate independently in various environments. The system's performance was assessed in real-world conditions, demonstrating an average speed of 0.5 m/s, an energy consumption of 0.2 kWh, and a decision-making error rate below 5%. The results suggest that the use of heterogeneous computing architectures can lead to the development of affordable and efficient autonomous navigation systems. The paper concludes by discussing potential future research directions and the broader implications of these findings for the field of autonomous systems.

Neuroscience

Asynchronous Impulse based Spiking Neural Network Framework using Rust Language
Raynhardt van Zyl

This project aims to develop an asynchronous impulse-based spiking neural network (SNN) framework using the Rust programming language. SNNs are third-generation neural networks that mimic the behavior of neurons in the human brain. Unlike traditional artificial neural networks, SNNs communicate by means of spikes, which are discrete events that take place at points in time, making them highly efficient for certain types of computation.

The Rust programming language is chosen for its performance and memory safety, especially its emphasis on concurrency, which is a key aspect of SNNs. The goal is to create a framework that can handle large-scale, real-time simulations of SNNs, with an emphasis on performance and accuracy.

The project will involve designing and implementing the SNN model, developing efficient algorithms for spike propagation and synaptic weight adjustment, and creating a user-friendly API for defining and training SNNs. The project will also explore the use of GPU acceleration to further enhance the performance of the SNN simulations.

Farming

Self-Sufficient Aquaponics System
Raynhardt van Zyl and El-mari Meyer

I am currently engaged in the development of an advanced, self-sufficient Aquaponics system. This project represents an intersection of hydroponics and aquaculture, leveraging the symbiotic relationships between aquatic life and plants to create a closed-loop, sustainable farming system.

The system is further enhanced by the integration of vertical farming techniques, maximizing the spatial efficiency and yield per square foot. The incorporation of artificial light sources, specifically tailored to the photosynthetic needs of the plants, allows for optimized growth rates independent of external weather conditions.

A key innovation in this project is the implementation of real-time nutrient and moisture sensing capabilities. Utilizing advanced sensor technology and data analytics, the system can dynamically adjust water and nutrient delivery based on the immediate needs of the plants. This not only ensures optimal growth conditions but also minimizes resource wastage.

The overarching objective of this project is to pioneer a new paradigm in sustainable agriculture. By creating a farming system that maximizes yield while minimizing resource usage, we can address some of the most pressing challenges in food security and environmental sustainability. This endeavor aligns with the broader scientific and technological trends towards precision agriculture and smart farming.

Power

Solar Tracking System
Raynhardt van Zyl and Vincent Van Zyl

I am currently in the process of developing an innovative solar tracking system. This project aims to optimize the efficiency of solar installations, particularly in regions far from the equator where the sun's angle varies significantly throughout the day.

The system employs advanced sensor technology and control algorithms to dynamically adjust the orientation of the solar panels, ensuring they remain perpendicular to the sun's rays at all times. This maximizes the incident solar radiation on the panels, thereby enhancing the overall energy output.

The project also incorporates a predictive model based on astronomical data, which allows the system to anticipate the sun's trajectory and adjust the panels accordingly. This proactive approach reduces the need for constant adjustments and minimizes wear and tear on the mechanical components.

The development of this solar tracking system represents a significant advancement in renewable energy technology. By optimizing the efficiency of solar installations, we can harness a greater proportion of the sun's energy, reducing our reliance on fossil fuels and contributing to a more sustainable energy future.

This project aligns with the broader scientific and technological trends towards smart and efficient energy systems. It underscores the potential of innovative engineering solutions to address some of the most pressing challenges in energy sustainability.

Finance

Algorithmic Trading System
Raynhardt van Zyl

I am currently developing an advanced algorithmic trading system designed to predict stock market trends and execute trades automatically. This project represents a cutting-edge application of machine learning and artificial intelligence in the field of financial technology.

The system is built on a novel transformer-based architecture, which is a base model of Llama fine-tuned on market data. This architecture allows the model to capture complex patterns and dependencies in the data, enabling it to make highly accurate predictions about future market movements.

In addition to market data, the system also incorporates news summaries as input. This allows the model to consider a broader range of factors influencing market trends, including geopolitical events, economic indicators, and company news. By integrating these diverse data sources, the system can make more informed and accurate trading decisions.

The development of this algorithmic trading system represents a significant advancement in the field of financial technology. By automating the trading process, the system can execute trades more quickly and accurately than human traders, potentially leading to higher returns.

This project aligns with the broader trends towards automation and data-driven decision making in finance. It underscores the potential of machine learning and artificial intelligence to transform traditional industries and create new opportunities for innovation and efficiency.
